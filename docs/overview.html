Al Noor Farm – Website and POS Overview

Simple summary of the storefront, payments, admin, POS, and Square sync

Introduction

We are building a custom website and point‑of‑sale (POS) system for Al Noor Farm. It connects online and in‑store sales, is easy to use, and will gradually replace the current Square setup.

Public Website – Browse and Buy

Customers can browse halal meats, eggs, and other goods with photos, descriptions, and prices, then add to cart and purchase online at their convenience. Clear categories make it easy for anyone to shop.

Online Ordering and Secure Card Payment

Checkout is familiar and secure with credit/debit cards. After payment, customers get confirmation and you receive instant notifications. Faster, low‑friction checkout improves satisfaction and retention.

Reference: checkout best‑practice insights (checkoutchamp.com)

Admin Dashboard – Products and Inventory

A private admin allows authorized staff to add/edit products, prices, and stock. Changes reflect immediately on the site. Inventory updates in real time across online and in‑store sales to prevent overselling.

References: makewebbetter.com (single source of truth), litextension.com (real‑time sync)

In‑Store POS

Use a tablet or computer to scan barcodes or pick products quickly. The system calculates totals, taxes, and discounts, accepts cash and cards, and updates inventory instantly. Receipts can be printed or emailed.

Square Integration (Transition)

Where possible, integrate Square APIs/SDKs so you can keep using Square hardware and see transactions in Square while also recording them here. If real‑time sync isn’t feasible, provide import/export fallbacks.

Reference: squareup.com (hardware and payments integration)

Benefits

  • Faster checkout online and in‑store → happier, repeat customers.
  • Unified inventory and no double entry → fewer errors and time saved.
  • Simple management and unified reporting → one source of truth.

Conclusion

The integrated storefront and POS deliver fast checkout, accurate inventory, and simple daily operations. With Square integration where possible, the transition is smooth and familiar.
