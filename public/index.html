Farm History

Al Noor Farm began when Amina and Yusuf Ahmed purchased a small pasture in 2010 to provide halal meat and eggs for family and friends. The demand for carefully raised, transparently sourced food grew quickly, and within two years the Ahmeds relocated to our current 18-acre home on Dickersonville Road.

The farm has expanded steadily ever since. We renovated a historic barn into a year-round brooder, installed a licensed processing kitchen, and added covered paddocks that let birds enjoy sunshine while staying protected from Western New York weather.

Today the farm combines traditional husbandry with modern record-keeping. Rotational grazing keeps our pastures healthy, rainwater catchment supplies our wash stations, and the online store connects customers from Buffalo to Niagara Falls with the same freshness neighbors enjoy at the farm gate.

Milestones

  • 2010 — Purchased the first laying hens and began weekend farm-gate sales.
  • 2012 — Moved to Ransomville and opened the original on-site farm store.
  • 2016 — Added mobile range coops and halal-certified processing equipment.
  • 2019 — Expanded into CSA shares and weekly deliveries around Buffalo.
  • 2023 — Launched the online storefront and POS to streamline ordering.

Mission & Values

Our mission is to nourish our community with halal, humanely raised food that honors faith, family, and the environment. Every flock is cared for by hand, every order is prepared to spec, and every visitor leaves knowing exactly how their food was raised.

Transparency is woven through everything we do—from ingredient lists and weight-based pricing to opening our gates for tours and workshops. When customers shop the farm store or meet us at market, they join the extended Al Noor family.

What guides every decision

  • Stewardship. Rotational grazing, composting, and native plantings protect our soil.
  • Nourishment. Birds enjoy non-GMO feed, clean water, and space to roam every day.
  • Access. Online ordering, delivery routes, and EBT-friendly pricing welcome every household.
  • Community. Farm tours, cooking demos, and donations support neighbors in need.

Meet the Ahmed Family

Al Noor Farm is run by three generations working side by side. The Ahmed family lives on-site, walks the fields before sunrise, and knows each flock by name. Whether hosting school visits or preparing weekend orders, the farm is an extension of our home.

We believe customers deserve to meet the people who care for their food. That is why you will find us answering questions on social media, greeting visitors at the farm store, and personally loading every delivery van.

Amina Ahmed

Founder & Farm Steward

Amina oversees the laying hens and broilers, schedules processing days, and keeps the farm compliant with halal, state, and federal requirements. Her background in food science ensures every batch meets exacting standards.

Yusuf Ahmed

Livestock & Pasture Manager

Yusuf designs the rotational grazing plan, repairs infrastructure, and leads weekend farm tours. He coordinates deliveries across Niagara County so every order arrives chilled and on time.

Layla Ahmed

Market & Community Lead

Layla runs the online store, organizes CSA communications, and develops recipes that help customers cook confidently. She also partners with local mosques and shelters for donation drives.
